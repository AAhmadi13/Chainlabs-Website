What is Blockchain Technology?



Benefits of Blockchain Technology

Security

Blockchain offers reliable security through ‘Cryptography’ and ‘Decentralisation’ processes. One data is recorded onto a blockchain which is nearly impossible to customise or erase; this makes it very reliant and resistant to fraud and unauthorised access.


Cost Efficiency

By eliminating intermediaries and automating processes through smart contracts, blockchain can reduce transaction costs and streamline operations. This is particularly beneficial in sectors like finance and supply chain management.


Decentralisation

There is no one authority or weak spot in the realm of blockchain technology since it can only operate on distributed networks of computers. Increasing trust and strength via decentralisation makes it more difficult for unauthorised access or system breakdowns to occur.


Disadvantages of Blockchain Technology


Energy Consumption

Some blockchain systems, like Bitcoin, use a lot of energy for things like mining and processing transactions. This has raised questions about whether it's bad for the environment. That's what some blockchain networks are like. They're using tons of energy, and that's not great for the planet. It's not just the energy, it's also the pollution it causes. This is a big problem, especially now that we're all trying to be more eco-friendly.


Lack of Regulation

The clear lack of regulations in terms of jurisdictions can end up in legal and complaint issues for blockchain projects. This possibility can significantly decrease the investment in blockchain technology.


Complexity

Blockchain technology is like a digital ledger that keeps a secure record of transactions. Imagine it as a shared digital notebook where you can write down who sent what to whom. This notebook is not stored in one place; it's duplicated across many computers, making it hard to tamper with.

To set up and use this digital notebook, you need some technical skills, like knowing how to write in a special code. Also, you need a bunch of computers working together, which can be costly.

For regular people and organisations, It's powerful but not always necessary for everyday tasks. So, it's important to think carefully before diving into blockchain technology, as it can be a bit like using a sledgehammer to crack a nut in some situations.


How are Blockchains Used?

Blocks on the blockchain of Bitcoin, as we now know, store transactional data. On a blockchain today, there are over 23,000 alternative cryptocurrency systems. However, it turns out that preserving information about other kinds of transactions on blockchain is dependable.

Among the businesses exploring with blockchain include Siemens, Walmart, Pfizer, AIG, and Unilever. For instance, IBM developed the Food Trust blockchain to track the path taken by food goods as they are delivered to their destinations.

Why do this action? Numerous E. Coli, salmonella, and listeria outbreaks have occurred in the food sector; in certain instances, dangerous substances were inadvertently added to food. It has previously taken weeks to identify the origin of these epidemics or the reason why individuals get unwell.

Brands may follow a food product's journey from its starting point to every stop it takes, all the way to delivery, by using blockchain technology. Furthermore, these businesses can now see everything else they could have come into touch with, which makes it possible to identify issues far earlier and possibly save lives. There are other ways that blockchain may be implemented; this is just one example of how it's done in practice.
